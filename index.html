Phase 02 — Supplementation

Why isolate,
not concentrate.

For a vegetarian recomp plate already carrying dairy and legumes, isolate fills the gap without adding lactose load or extra fat.

MetricIsolateConcentrate
Protein by weight90–95%70–80%
Lactose content~0–1g3–5g
Fat per scoop<1g1–3g
Digestion speedFastModerate
Best timingPost-liftAnytime
Cost per gram proteinHigherLower

The filtration difference, in practice

Whey isolate is filtered further than concentrate, stripping out most of the lactose and fat that ride along in a standard scoop. What's left is closer to pure protein — useful when your vegetarian meals already lean on dairy for their own protein load.

For body recomposition specifically, isolate's speed of absorption matters more right after training, when amino acids need to reach muscle tissue fast to support the simultaneous build-and-burn the diet is asking the body to do.

It's not a requirement — concentrate works fine across most of the day — but isolate earns its place in the one or two shakes timed around your lift.
